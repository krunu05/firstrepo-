MANAGAV

Mana Village, located in Uttarakhand's Chamoli district, is prominently featured in the image and is known as the "First Indian Village" near the Indo-Tibetan border. This scenic village holds significant cultural and spiritual importance:

Location and Features: Situated at an altitude of approximately 3,200 meters (10,500 feet), it is just about 3 km from the sacred Badrinath Temple and sits on the banks of the Saraswati River. It is surrounded by the breathtaking Himalayan peaks.

Historical and Mythological Significance: Mana Village is believed to be connected to the Mahabharata, with legends stating that the Pandavas passed through here on their journey to heaven. It is also home to the Vyas Gufa and Ganesh Gufa caves, where sage Vyasa is believed to have composed the Mahabharata.

Tourism: Designated as a "Tourism Village" by the Uttarakhand government, it serves as a starting point for various trekking routes, including the trek to Vasudhara Falls. It is also known for its local produce like kidney beans and potatoes.
