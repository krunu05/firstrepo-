My Favorite Travel Destination

Mana, also known as Mana Gaon, is a village in Uttarakhand, India, recognized as the "first village of India" and located near the Indo-Tibetan border. It is situated just 3 km from the Badrinath temple and is a popular destination for pilgrims and tourists. The village is known for its scenic beauty, including the Saraswati River, Vasundhara Falls, and the Bheem Pul. It's also rich in mythological significance, with connections to the Mahabharata and the legend of Vyas Gufa (Vyas's cave).
